Timeline of GMO Usage in the United States

This timeline aims to help others see the chronological events that lead to current gmos

Timeline

1935

Russian scientist Andrei Nikolaevitch Belozersky isolated pure DNA

1973

The First Man-Made Strands of Recombinant DNA

1975

The Asilomar Confrence takes place and guidlines are created for safe use of genetic engineering

1980

A 5-to-4 Supreme Court ruling leades to the first GMO patent being issued for a bacterium that eats crude oil

1982

The FDA approves it's first GMO, Humulin, insulin produced by genetically modified E. coli

1994

The U.S. Food and Drug Administration approves the Flavr Savr tomato for general sale in Grocery Stores

1996

The first glyphosate resistant weeds start popping up with immunities of 7 to 11 times stronger than that of the standard susceptible population

1997

The European Union rules in favor of mandatory labelling of all GMO food products, including animal feed

1999

GMO crops dominate the international agricultural market place with over 100 million acres that were planted worldwide

2003

GMO resistant pests start appearing, mainly the Bt-toxin-resistant caterpillar-cum-moth, Helicoverpa zea, who in less than a decade adapted to feast on GMO Bt cotton crops

2007

European Food Safety Authority concludes that GM plants do not pose a relevent rick to human or animal health or to the environment

2009

Monsanto introduces their line of new Roundup2 seeds

2011

Research in eastern Quebec finds Bt toxins in the blood of pregnant women and show evidence of passing the blood-brain barrier

2012

French farmer Paul Francois sues Monsanto for chemical posining he says was caused by the Roundup Ready product Lasso

2014

Monsanto's GMO Patents for their Roundup Ready line of seeds are beginning to expire, but their Roundup2 product line takes over

2015

Scientist discover more genes that trigger food allergies and are able to isolate them as well as providing crops with more nutritional value
